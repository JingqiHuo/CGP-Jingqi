Our Data

The data used to produce maps in this website is provided below. This data has been made available to allow – and indeed, encourage – other parties to explore the information and carry out their own analyses.

  • Download our tree Data (roseburntrees.csv)
    • roseburntrees.csv contains the coordinates, species type, and dryweight biomass for sampled trees. The first three rows of the file contain metadata. Users can explore this data in geospatial software of their choosing; an example of how this can be done in QGIS is provided.

    How to import csv file into your GIS software?

    Step.1 Download the tree data file.
    Step.2 Open 'Delimited Text' tab, choose the csv file, then click 'add'.

    Step.3 Check our plots on your computer!

  • Download the Result of Biomass and Carbon (.tif files in a .zip)

    • roseburnbiomass.tif provides aboveground biomass density throughout the study area. Units: kg/m2. Projection: WGS 84 Pseudo-Mercator (EPSG:3857)
    roseburncarbon.tif provides estimated carbon throughout the study area. Units: kg. Projection: Pseudo-Mercator (EPSG:3857)

  • Download Boundary and Tramline Data (.zip)

    • roseburn_path_removal.kmz shows the area of path that would be lost if the tramline proposal were to proceed (as estimated by the project team, using Google Earth Pro).
    tramline.gpkg shows a section of the proposed tramline (as defined the City of Edinburgh Council).
