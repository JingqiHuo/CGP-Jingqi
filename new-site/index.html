CGS-Group3: Carbon Sink
Proposal Tramline

In February 2024, the City of Edinburgh Council proposed an extension to the existing tram network. Much attention has since been drawn to the Roseburn Path – the favoured tramline would replace this ecological corridor and treasured active travel route.
Interested in the debate being generated, a team from the University of Edinburgh sought to quantify the impacts of removing this path. Focusing upon carbon loss, a ‘ground up’ approach was adopted to estimate its total carbon stock. This first required estimating biomass at the individual tree level; the map to the left shows a subset of the trees sampled (only those trees that informed the final results). A considerable range of species can be observed, with each map icon providing more information on each tree.

Using this field data, in addition to high-resolution LiDAR data, biomass estimations were upscaled to the entirety of the path. (For further information around methodology, please refer to the formal report produced). It is estimated that the path contains 1,469,366kg of biomass. Using the widely held assumption that ~50% of aboveground biomass is carbon, removing the path could result in a carbon stock loss of 734,683kg.
